## 1. Data Source

* Windows Security Logs
* Event IDs:

  * **4625** → Failed Logon
  * **4624** → Successful Logon
* Collected via:

  * Splunk Universal Forwarder

---

## 2. Detection Logic

* Multiple failed login attempts (4625) followed by a successful login (4624)
* Indicates potential brute-force attack success

---

## 3. Splunk Query

```spl
index=main (EventCode=4625 OR EventCode=4624)
| eval status=if(EventCode==4625,"Failed","Success")
| timechart span=1m count by status
| fillnull value=0
```

## 4. Analysis

* High volume of **Failed (4625)** events indicates brute-force attempts
* A **Success (4624)** event following failures suggests:

  * Credentials were successfully guessed
* Time-based visualization reveals:

  * Attack intensity
  * Exact moment of compromise

---

## 5. Example from Lab

* Attacker IP: **192.168.1.106**
* Target System: **192.168.1.107**
* Observed Pattern:

  * Multiple failed login attempts
  * Followed by a successful login

---

## 6. Detection Insight

This pattern may indicate:

* Brute-force attack
* Credential stuffing
* Unauthorized access

---

## 7. Advanced Filtering (Optional)

Focus on specific attacker IP:

```spl
index=main (EventCode=4625 OR EventCode=4624) Source_Network_Address=192.168.1.106
| eval status=if(EventCode==4625,"Failed","Success")
| timechart span=1m count by status
```
