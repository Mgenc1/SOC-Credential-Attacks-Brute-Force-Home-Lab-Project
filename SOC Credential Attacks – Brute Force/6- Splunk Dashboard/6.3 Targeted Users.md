## 1. Data Source

* Log Source: Windows Security Event Logs
* Collection Method: Splunk Universal Forwarder
* Relevant Event IDs:

  * **4625** – Failed Logon Attempt
  * **4624** – Successful Logon

---

## 2. Analytical Approach

This analysis focuses on failed authentication events (Event ID 4625) to determine which accounts are being targeted by an attacker. A high frequency of failed attempts against a specific account indicates focused attack behavior.

Additionally, correlation with successful logon events (4624) is used to determine whether the targeted account was ultimately compromised.

---

## 3. Splunk Query

```spl
index=main EventCode=4625
| stats count as attempt_count by Account_Name
| sort - attempt_count
| head 10
```

---

## 4. Findings

Analysis of the dataset reveals that the account **"testuser"** was the primary target of repeated authentication attempts.

* Source IP: **192.168.1.106**
* Attack Pattern:

  * Multiple failed logon attempts (4625)
  * Followed by a successful logon (4624)

This pattern strongly indicates a successful brute-force attack against a single user account.

---

## 5. Detection Insight

The observed behavior is consistent with:

* **Brute Force Attack (MITRE ATT&CK T1110)**

  * Repeated login attempts against a single account
* Not consistent with password spraying, which typically involves:

  * Low attempt count across multiple accounts

---

## 6. Correlation Opportunity

To strengthen detection, this panel should be correlated with:

* **06.02 Failed vs Successful Login Trend**

  * To identify the exact moment of compromise
* **06.01 Top Attacking IPs**

  * To attribute attack source

---

## 7. Advanced Analysis

To analyze attacker-user relationships:

```spl
index=main EventCode=4625
| stats count by Source_Network_Address, Account_Name
| sort - count
```

This allows identification of which attacker IP targets which accounts.

---

## 8. Risk Assessment

* Targeted Account: **testuser**
* Attack Success: **Confirmed**
* Risk Level: **High**

Reason:

* High volume of failed attempts
* Successful authentication observed
* Remote source IP involved

---

This panel provides:
