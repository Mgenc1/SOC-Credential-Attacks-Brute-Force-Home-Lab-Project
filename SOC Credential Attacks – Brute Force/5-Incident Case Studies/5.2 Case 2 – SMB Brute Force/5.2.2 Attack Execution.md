### 1. Attack Vector
- **Protocol:** SMB (Server Message Block)  
- **Target Port:** 445  
- **Method:** Credential brute force  
- The attacker attempted to guess the `testuser` password via repeated login attempts.

---

### 2. Tools / Techniques
- **Automated Brute Force Tools:** Detected processes suggest PowerShell automation.
  - Process observed: `splunk-powershell.exe` (used to execute automated scripts)
  - Parent Process: `splunkd.exe`  
- Tools commonly used for this type of attack: Hydra, CrackMapExec, Ncrack (Sysmon rules monitored these).

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Execution
1. **Initial Connection:** The attacker initiated TCP connections to port 445 of the target host.
2. **Login Attempts:** Multiple failed logons recorded (Event 4625), indicating brute force attempts on the `testuser` account.
3. **Successful Compromise:** After repeated attempts, one login succeeded (Event 4624), confirming that credentials were obtained or guessed correctly.
4. **Post-Compromise Verification:** Network connections (Sysmon Event 3) confirm that the attacker established communication over SMB, potentially for data access or lateral movement.

---

### 4. Observed Indicators
- **Source IP:** 192.168.1.106  
- **Destination Host:** `umut`  
- **Destination Port:** 445  
- **Logon Type:** 3 (Network)  
- **Process Execution:** PowerShell automation detected (Sysmon Event 1)  

**Interpretation:**  
The attack followed a standard SMB brute force pattern:
- Recon/connection → multiple failed logins → successful login → persistent network access.

---

### 5. Notes
- No evidence of privilege escalation was observed post-compromise.
- Attack appears to be **credential-focused** rather than exploiting vulnerabilities in SMB itself.
