## 1️. Attack Patterns Observed
- **Credential-based attacks**:
  - Multiple failed logins (Event ID 4625) followed by a successful login (Event ID 4624) indicate brute-force activity.
  - Targeted usernames were often default or weak accounts.
- **Post-login behavior**:
  - Sysmon Event IDs 1 and 10 captured post-login process creation and LSASS access attempts.
  - Indicates potential attempts to escalate privileges or maintain persistence.
- **Source IP behavior**:
  - Repeated access from a single IP (192.168.1.106) highlights the importance of correlating logs to identify attacking hosts.

---

## 2️. Detection & Response Insights
- **Failed vs Successful Login correlation** is critical to detect potential account compromise.
- **Log enrichment** with Sysmon events improves visibility of post-login malicious activity.
- **Alerts and dashboards** in Splunk allowed rapid identification of attacking IPs and targeted users.
- **Event timeline analysis** helps correlate cross-case activity, revealing broader attack patterns.

---

## 3️. Gaps & Improvements
- **Initial detection delays** due to insufficient correlation between failed and successful login events.
- **Limited MFA enforcement** on lab accounts increased attack success probability.
- **Network segmentation and firewall rules** were not fully tested; implementing these would further limit attack surface.

---

**Summary:**  
The lab demonstrated that combining **log correlation, monitoring, and proactive alerting** is effective for detecting brute-force attacks. Continuous improvement in detection logic, dashboard design, and incident response procedures strengthens SOC capabilities and provides a realistic practice environment for security analysts.
