### 1️. Account & Access Policies
- **Enforce strong password policies**: Minimum length 12, complexity requirements, periodic rotation.
- **Implement account lockout thresholds**: Temporarily lock accounts after 3–5 failed logon attempts.
- **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** for remote access and privileged accounts.
- **Restrict login hours** for sensitive accounts to reduce exposure to attacks outside business hours.

---

### 2️. Network & Perimeter Controls
- **Limit RDP/SMB exposure**:
  - Restrict access to internal IP ranges or via VPN.
  - Close unnecessary SMB ports to untrusted networks.
- **Deploy firewall rules**: Block known malicious IPs and rate-limit login attempts.
- **Segment networks**: Reduce lateral movement if a host is compromised.

---

### 3️. Monitoring & Detection Enhancements
- **Sysmon & Security Event Logging**:
  - Continue capturing Event IDs 4624, 4625, 4648, and Sysmon 1, 3, 10.
  - Correlate failed vs successful logins for early detection.
- **Splunk Dashboards**:
  - Implement alerts for unusual login frequency per account or source IP.
  - Track post-login process activity for anomalous behavior.
- **Threat Intelligence Integration**:
  - Subscribe to threat feeds for known attacker IPs targeting SMB/RDP.
  - Automate blocking via Splunk alert actions.

---

### 4️. Endpoint & System Hardening
- Keep Windows and software **fully patched**.
- Disable or restrict local admin accounts wherever possible.
- Audit and review **privilege changes** to prevent post-compromise escalation.

---

**Summary:**  
By combining **policy enforcement, network segmentation, monitoring, and proactive alerting**, the risk of brute-force attacks and post-compromise activity can be significantly reduced. These measures also support continuous improvement in incident detection and SOC maturity.
